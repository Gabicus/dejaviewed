There Is No Democracy Without Journalism Scott Pelley was asked about President Trump reportedly calling him a “stiff” and suggesting he was among people who “don’t care about the country.” His response wasn’t about politics. It was about service. Pelley pointed to years spent covering wars alongside American troops in Iraq, Afghanistan, and Kuwait before making a broader point: “There is no democracy without journalism. It can’t be done.” Whether you agree with a journalist’s reporting or not, a free press is one of the institutions that allows citizens to hold power accountable. The danger comes when we begin treating information like a menu—accepting facts from people we like and dismissing them from people we don’t.
