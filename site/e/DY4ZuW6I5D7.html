1k ft — Deep-dive / try it

The first sentence of your artist statement is the reason nobody reads the second one. It is almost never the work. It is that the opening line sounds like every other statement a gallery has read that week, so they switch off before they reach the part that matters. Three openers to retire today. I am an emerging artist. They can see that. It tells them nothing. My work explores. Explore is the word we use when we have not decided what the work actually is. Oil on canvas. The medium is not a reason to keep reading. Your first line should be the sentence you would say out loud if someone asked what you make. Plain and specific.
