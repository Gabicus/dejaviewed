Traditional Appalachian version of House of the Rising Sun. 🌅🪕 Did you know this was a traditional tune sung in the mountains? While the world knows the 6/8 rock version, the oldest recordings—like those from Roscoe Holcomb—carry this driving 4/4 rhythm. It feels less like a ballad from a bar and more like a dire warning from the hills. While the world knows the 6/8 rock version, some of the oldest versions, like Roscoe Holcomb’s, carry this driving 4/4 rhythm that feels more like a dire warning from the hills than a ballad from the bar.
