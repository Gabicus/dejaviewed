Interesting fact: When you press Ctrl+C in a terminal, your shell sends a SIGINT (Signal Interrupt, signal #2) to the foreground process group — not just the process itself. The process can actually catch or ignore SIGINT using signal handlers, which is why some programs like vim or custom scripts don't immediately die when you press it. For a guaranteed kill, you'd escalate to SIGKILL (signal #9) via kill -9 <PID>, which cannot be caught, blocked, or ignored by any process. Have you ever had Ctrl+C fail on you mid-script — and did you know why?
