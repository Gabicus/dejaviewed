There are five numbers that decide what your work is actually worth. Most artists guess all five, then blame the market. It is not your talent. It is your maths. One. Your size number. Height plus width in inches, times one rate you set and use on everything. Two. Your cost floor. Materials, framing, and the commission a gallery takes, often half. Clear it or you are paying to give the work away. Three. Your comparable. Three artists with a CV like yours. Not the ones you admire. The ones level with you. That band is your real market. Four. Your consistency number. The same formula on every piece, so a collector can predict your prices. Random numbers read as an artist who does not know their own worth. Five.
