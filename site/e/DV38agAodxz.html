1k ft — Deep-dive / try it

Spam calls are one of the most annoying problems on the internet era. But surprisingly, a U.S. law from 1991 might allow you to earn up to $1,500 for each illegal robocall. The problem is that most robocallers hide behind fake names, spoofed phone numbers, and anonymous operations, making it almost impossible for consumers to identify them. That’s where technology - and increasingly AI - comes in. Tools like DoNotPay created a virtual credit card system designed to uncover the real identity of spam callers. When a robocaller tries to sell you something, you give them the virtual card. When they attempt to process the payment, the payment network reveals the business name, address, and phone number behind the call.
